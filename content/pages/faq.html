General Questions

  • Is OpenWISP suitable for me?

    OpenWISP is ideal for small teams managing large networks on a budget, particularly those who prefer open-source, free software solutions. It is well-suited for organizations that prioritize open-source network management, companies building custom products, and users in emerging markets seeking affordable solutions.

    For sufficiently large networks, traditional network management vendors often require significant investment, while OpenWISP can achieve similar goals at a fraction of the cost, while also offering greater flexibility and customization.

    Organizations deploying hotspot networks, municipal WiFi, or those familiar with Linux OpenWrt will find OpenWISP especially useful.

    However, OpenWISP may not be the best fit for very small networks (fewer than 20 devices), organizations lacking IT expertise, or enterprises seeking open-source alternatives solely for cost-saving purposes.

  • What hardware can OpenWISP control?

    Any OpenWrt compatible hardware.

Technical Questions

  • Can OpenWISP work with devices that do not run OpenWrt?

    Yes, OpenWISP can work with devices that do not run OpenWrt, but some features may require adaptation. OpenWISP supports standard protocols like SSH, SNMP, and APIs, which allow it to manage different types of network devices. While OpenWrt offers tighter integration and broader compatibility with all of OpenWISP's features, other devices can still benefit from the platformâ€™s monitoring, IP management, and automation capabilities with appropriate configuration.

  • Does OpenWISP support the management of hotspot networks?

    Certainly! OpenWISP is widely recognized as an open-source solution for WiFi Hotspot Management in public Wi-Fi environments.

    For detailed insights and functionalities regarding hotspot management, please refer to the WiFi Hotspot & Captive Portal page.

  • Can OpenWISP be used to manage mesh networks?

    Absolutely! OpenWISP offers robust support for managing mesh networks, providing essential tools for configuration, monitoring, and maintenance.

    For comprehensive guidance on setting up and optimizing wireless mesh networks, refer to the How to Set Up a Wireless Mesh Network page.
