History of OpenWISP

From bridging the digital divide to building a versatile network management solution.

2008

The Birth of ProvinciaWiFi

In the summer of 2008, the Province of Rome embarked on an ambitious mission to bridge the digital divide by providing free public Wi-Fi across its expansive territory, encompassing Rome and 120 other cities, serving approximately 4.5 million inhabitants. The project, initially known as ProvinciaWiFi and later renamed WiFi Metropolitano, aimed to democratize access to ICT for all citizens.

Embracing Openness

From its inception, ProvinciaWiFi embraced an open philosophy, advocating for open software, operating systems, and knowledge sharing. Collaborating with the university consortium CASPUR, the project pursued innovative methodologies for network distribution, enabling the utilization of non-dedicated network infrastructures for public connectivity services.

2009

Adoption by other Italian municipalities

Throughout 2009, OpenWISP played a pivotal role in the expansion of ProvinciaWiFi, transforming it into a network boasting thousands of active hotspots and users daily. The success of the collaboration between the Province of Rome and CASPUR paved the way for adoption by municipalities and universities across Italy.

2015

OpenWISP: A Global Endeavor

By 2015, OpenWISP had evolved into a comprehensive Network Management System, addressing the shortcomings of its predecessor and catering to a global audience. With a focus on reusability, modularity, and extensibility, OpenWISP became a beacon for developers and engineers worldwide, fostering community contributions and facilitating internet expansion in emerging countries.

Today

Embracing the Future

Today, OpenWISP continues to evolve, empowering communities and organizations to build robust, accessible networks that transcend geographical boundaries. With its commitment to openness, innovation, and inclusivity, OpenWISP remains at the forefront of connectivity, driving positive change in the digital landscape.
